People infatuated with money are vile and baseless.

The obsession with wealth often masks a deeper emptiness. When money becomes the only measure of worth, it strips away the humanity behind every choice.

Attraction to money can be a tool rather than a purpose, but once it becomes an idol, it breeds self-centeredness, entitlement, and a disregard for people around you. The problem is not the currency itself, it is the hollow values that follow.

Those who chase wealth at the expense of integrity and empathy create a world where relationships are transactional, and compassion is sacrificed for accumulation. Such an approach is inherently fragile and self-defeating.

A more grounded outlook recognizes that money is useful, but not sacred. Real value lies in the work we do, the people we help, and the sense of meaning we carry beyond our bank balance.

Calling the infatuation with money 'vile' is harsh, but it is a necessary wake-up call: we should question the cultural narrative that glorifies wealth above character and treat financial success as a byproduct of purpose rather than an end in itself.
